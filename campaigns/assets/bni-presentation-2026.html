Decoration Technique 01

Embroidery

Thread stitched directly into fabric. The gold standard for professional, long-lasting branding.

Best For

Polos, jackets, caps, bags - anything that needs to look premium

Durability

Outlasts the garment itself. Won't fade, crack or peel with washing

How It Works

Digital file converted to stitch pattern. Machine stitches thread into fabric at high speed

Stitch Count

Pricing based on stitch count. Typical logo: 5,000 - 15,000 stitches

Your screen print photo
Decoration Technique 02

Screen Printing

Ink pushed through a mesh screen onto fabric. The go-to method for bulk t-shirt and apparel orders.

Best For

T-shirts, hoodies, tote bags - large runs at low per-unit cost

Volume Sweet Spot

50+ pieces. Setup cost spread across the run makes bulk orders very economical

How It Works

One screen per color. Ink applied through stencil. Each color = one pass through the press

Colors

1-6 spot colors typical. Each extra color = extra screen = extra cost

Decoration Technique 03

Heat Transfer

Design printed onto transfer paper, then heat-pressed onto fabric. Great for full-color, detailed graphics.

Best For

Full-color logos, photo-realistic prints, small-to-medium runs

Versatility

Works on cotton, polyester, blends. No color limits - full CMYK printing

How It Works

Design printed on special paper, then transferred using a heat press at 350-400 degrees F

Types

Vinyl cut (simple), digital transfer (detailed), DTF (direct to film - newest)

Decoration Technique 04

Direct to Garment
(DTG)

Inkjet printing directly onto fabric. Think of it as a printer for t-shirts - unlimited colors, zero setup.

Best For

Complex artwork, photos, gradients. One-offs and small runs (1-50 pieces)

No Setup Cost

No screens, no plates. Print one shirt or one hundred at the same per-unit quality

How It Works

Garment pre-treated, loaded into printer. Water-based inks sprayed directly onto fabric

Fabric

Best on 100% cotton. Polyester and dark garments need special pre-treatment

Decoration Technique 05

Pad Printing

A silicone pad picks up ink from an etched plate and transfers it onto irregular surfaces. The secret behind branded pens and mugs.

Best For

Pens, golf balls, USB drives, bottles - hard, curved, or uneven surfaces

Precision

Extremely fine detail on small areas. Sharp logos even on tiny products

How It Works

Ink sits in etched plate. Silicone pad lifts ink, conforms to product shape, deposits cleanly

Colors

1-4 spot colors typical. Each color requires a separate plate and pass

Your sublimation photo
Decoration Technique 06

Sublimation

Ink turns to gas under heat and bonds permanently with polyester fibers. Edge-to-edge, all-over printing with zero texture.

Best For

All-over prints, sports jerseys, mugs, mousepads, phone cases

Feel

No hand feel at all - ink becomes part of the fabric. Won't crack, peel, or fade

How It Works

Printed on transfer paper with sublimation ink. Heat converts ink to gas, bonds to polyester

Limitation

Polyester or poly-coated surfaces only. Won't work on 100% cotton or dark fabrics

Decoration Technique 07

Laser Engraving

A focused laser beam etches your design permanently into metal, wood, leather, or glass. The ultimate in premium branding.

Best For

Executive gifts, awards, drinkware, metal pens, leather goods

Permanence

Cannot fade, wash off, or wear away. Lasts the lifetime of the product

How It Works

CO2 or fiber laser burns away surface material, creating a permanent mark at microscopic precision

Premium Feel

Single-color only, but the tactile depth and precision conveys quality like nothing else
