## The 7-Step Sales Process

The UFG franchise sales process has been refined over 40 years. **Never skip steps.** The shortcut is always the long way around. At each step, your only goal is to *get to the next step*.

### Step 1: Initial Contact (Goal: Book the First Meeting)
- **Keep it short.** Convey three things: briefly describe the business, confirm investment amount, get their timeframe.
- Don't answer too many questions — leave them wanting more.
- Call on the **same day** the lead comes in. Speed wins.
- If resistant to booking, clarify "no obligation — just information so you can make an informed decision."
- Best time to reach non-responders: Saturday 10am–noon, Sunday 6–8:30pm.

**Script (Initial Call):**
> "Hello, my name is Prakash. Is [prospect's name] available?"
>
> "Hi [name]. This is Prakash from Fully Promoted. I'm the Master Franchisee for Ireland. I just wanted to give you a quick call to introduce myself. I'd love to sit down with you to share some information about our brand and help you see whether this might be a good opportunity for you. We can also take a tour of our Dublin store."

**Narrowing the date:**
> "Do you prefer earlier in the week or later? Later — great. Wednesday or Thursday? What time of day — morning or afternoon?"

**Tips:**
- Don't offer Friday (reserve day for reschedules)
- Get multiple contact numbers
- Alternate between calls and messages over 7–10 days (4–5 attempts)

---

### Step 2: First Meeting / Tour (Goal: Provide Application & Set Up Second Meeting)
- Can be in-person or Zoom/webinar
- **Start with YOUR story** — explain how you came to this business (20 years in software, moved into promo industry, discovered franchise model)
- Ask THEIR story — open-ended questions:
  - How long have you been looking for your own business?
  - What made you decide to look?
  - How did you hear about our brand?
  - What aspects do you think you'd enjoy?
  - What are you looking to get out of a business?
- **Listen 75% of the time**
- Deliver the sales presentation conversationally — use real stories from franchise owners
- Add tasteful humor, keep eye contact

**After the presentation — lay down information in this order:**
1. **Franchise Disclosure Document** (email after meeting)
2. **Franchise Agreement** (place face-down on table, flip through briefly)
3. **Schedule A** (equipment package — point out key items)
4. **Personal Profile form** — "This is your next step" (flip toward them, explain pages)
   - Page 1: Basic info (name, address, phone)
   - Page 2: Background/experience
   - Page 3: "About You" — most important (motivations, concerns, areas of interest)
   - Page 4: Financial snapshot
   - Offer electronic version: "I can email you a link to fill it out online"

**Explain the Steps:**
1. Give them the Personal Profile (next step)
2. Follow up in a few days for second meeting (training & support focused)
3. Discovery Day Tour — meet executive team, learn culture
4. After DDT: either "great people but not for me" or "what's my next step?"
5. Fully refundable deposit (Binder) — shows sincere interest, reserves area, allows us to work for you
6. Help find location, source funding, enter Training School

**Stack handover order:** Personal Profile on top, then Schedule A, Franchise Agreement, Brochure.

---

### Step 3: Second Meeting (Goal: Collect Application & Set Up Discovery Day)
- Schedule **within 2–3 days** of first meeting
- Confirm they've completed the Personal Profile
- If not: "that's not a problem, but if you could spare 10–15 minutes before our meeting, that would be great"
- If new partner present: give them the full presentation too
- If possible, take them to tour another store

**Reviewing the Personal Profile:**
- Go through **page by page** — do NOT skip to financials
- Comment on items: "I see you've run your own business — that'll be helpful"
- Connect their motivations to the business: "I notice family time is important — one of the best parts of our business is quality of life"
- THEN review the financial section
- If finances look fine: "everything looks fine" — put it down
- If unclear on funding: "can you let me know what sources of capital you plan on using?"

**Introduce Discovery Day:**
> "The next step in our research process is to invite you to attend a Discovery Day Tour. There's no better way to understand the culture and character of a company than to take a day to meet the people who run it."

- Get 2–3 possible dates from prospect
- Coordinate with your team
- Request the 3rd Meeting Packet

---

### Step 4: Pre-DDT Meeting (Goal: Set Up Binder Meeting for After DDT)
- Schedule a **quality meeting** ahead of DDT — not just a courtesy call
- Share Welcome Letter and Itinerary
- **Critically: set the date for the Binder Meeting** — prospect should know this is the next step
- Binder Meeting should be **no more than 48 hours** after DDT
- Share with DDT team: Personal Profile, key messages to reinforce, likely questions

---

### Step 5: Discovery Day Tour (Goal: Executives Validate to Get the Deposit)
- Should be **validation** of everything so far, not first-time selling
- Prospect should have already seen at least 2 locations
- Can be done via Zoom — conversion rates are actually equal or better
- 12 key points:
  1. Understand each step of the funnel
  2. Candidate learns about Marketing, Support, Brand, Virtual Tour, company philosophy
  3. Cover YOUR section only — stay in your lane
  4. Practice regularly
  5. Treat every DDT like the first time
  6. If you don't know an answer, pass it to the right person
  7. Read the pre-DDT form — anticipate questions
  8. **Don't discuss territory or real estate during DDT** — let RVP handle post-DDT
  9. Ask genuine questions and listen
  10. Know your script, have a good story
  11. If prospect has concerns, address immediately — don't wait
  12. **No numbers or earnings claims on DDT!**

---

### Step 6: Binder Meeting (Goal: Set Up Timeline to Close)
- Meeting should go smoothly if steps followed
- Prospect should fully expect to put down the **fully-refundable deposit (Binder)**
- The Binder "allows us to start going to work for you" — real estate, financing, logistics

**The Timeline:**
- Start with: "What are the **top 3 locations** where you'd want to open?"
- Then: "In an ideal world, **when** would you like to start operating?"
- Work **backwards** from their preferred Grand Opening date
- Target: Franchise Agreement signed ("Become a Franchise Owner") **30 days from today**
- Timeline items: Deposit → Location Search → Funding → Location Review → Become Owner → Lease Negotiation → Lease Signing → Buildout → Training & Setup → Grand Opening

**To attend Training School, need 3 things:**
1. Be a franchisee (paid Franchise Fee + Equipment Deposit)
2. Secured lease on property
3. Location buildout complete or near-complete

**Landlords love franchises** — higher success rate, long-term tenant. Use this leverage for rent-free periods and buildout concessions.

---

### Step 7: Closing the Deal (Goal: Finish Strongly & Handover)
- If steps followed, closing is just coordination
- **Preliminary Location Search:** At Binder Meeting, give them the job of driving their area and finding 3–4 locations. You do the same.
- **Location Search Meeting:** 1 week after Binder — agree on Top 3, commence negotiations
- **75% of closings happen within 45 days of receiving the Binder**
- DocuSign works well for closing remotely
- Franchisee wires/EFTs the Franchise Fee for closing

**Best practice:** Close within 30 days of Binder, collect all money at close, no side letters.

## Sales Funnel Benchmarks

| Stage | Conversion Rate |
|-------|----------------|
| Passed Leads (to First Meeting) | 35% |
| Tour/Webinar Completed (to Application) | 50% |
| Applications (to Discovery Day) | 50% |
| Discovery Day (to Deposit/Binder) | 60% |
| Deposit (to Order/Close) | 70% |
| Order close rate | 80% |

**Work backwards from your sales goal** using these percentages to determine how many leads you need.

## Conversions (Existing Businesses Joining the Network)

**Benefits for Franchisor:**
- Immediate royalty stream (they already have customers)
- Bank/vendor confidence from network growth
- Adds capability and skills to network
- Opens up smaller territories
- Reinforces brand to negative franchisees

**Benefits for Franchisee (pitch to independent print/promo shops):**
- "If not me, who?" — someone in their town will join
- Systems and processes they don't have time to build
- Marketing strength (website, PPC, email, collateral)
- Support network and collective intelligence
- Group buying power from preferred vendors
- **Exit strategy** — selling as a franchise is much easier than selling independently

## Territory Planning

- Think long-term — mistakes now limit growth for 10–20 years
- Focus on **units of demand**, not just population or business count
- Generally offer **non-exclusive** territories (protected marketing areas)
- We won't open another territory in their area while they're our franchisee
- **Don't sell territory first** — get them excited about the business, then territory
- Beware negotiators asking for "first mover" concessions — don't "sell the farm"
- Offer **first right of refusal** on neighboring territory (with deposit + time limit, usually 1 year)
- Be transparent with first franchisees about growth plans

**Territory parameters (rule of thumb for B2B like Fully Promoted):**
- Larger territories than B2C (franchisees go to customers, not the other way)
- Consider: employing businesses count, population density, competitors

## Launch Process (After Closing)

1. Reset the Timeline — match expectations with reality
2. Professional handover call from sales to Franchise Logistics Coordinator (FLC)
3. FLC is single point of contact until business opens
4. **Head Start Program** — documents and processes for new business
5. Departments involved: Finance, Construction, Real Estate, Signage, Training, Purchasing, Launches, Marketing

**Typical Fully Promoted launch flow:**
Location Found & Lease Signed → Review Construction & Financing → Purchasing & Shipments → Pre-Setup → Training School → Tech Setup → Market Setup → Franchisee Start Date → Post Launch Review

## Resales

- Resales are **normal** — not to be feared
- Most people want to sell their business 3 times in the first year (it's overwhelming)
- If within first year, try to talk them out of selling — encourage persistence
- If genuine resale, it's an opportunity to breathe fresh life into a store

## Quick Tips Collection

- First 1–3 franchisees must be **well-funded** — under-funded franchisees create pressure and bad optics
- The shortcut is ALWAYS the long way around — stick to the steps
- Don't be the "Shell Answer Man" — don't answer every question on the first call
- Never say "confirm the meeting" — say "verify"
- Arrive 20 minutes early to meetings
- In the car after the tour: **stay silent** — let them speak first. Goal: hear "what's my next step?"
- Personal Profile form should be on **top of the stack** when handing materials
- Collect a Binder from as many prospects as possible — once they put money down, they're committed
- TIME KILLS DEALS — speed wins at every stage
- You are NEVER "sold out" of territories
