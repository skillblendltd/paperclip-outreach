Technique 01

Embroidery

Thread stitched into fabric. Premium feel that outlasts the garment.

Best For

Polos, jackets, caps, bags

Durability

Won't fade, crack, or peel

How It Works

Digital file to stitch pattern

Pricing

Stitch count. 5K-15K typical

Technique 02

Screen Printing

Ink through mesh onto fabric. The bulk order champion.

Best For

T-shirts, hoodies, tote bags

Sweet Spot

50+ pieces - cost spreads out

How It Works

One screen per color

Colors

1-6 spot. More = more cost

Technique 03

Heat Transfer / DTF

Full-color design pressed onto fabric. Vivid, detailed, versatile.

Best For

Full-color logos, photo prints

Versatility

Any fabric, no color limits

How It Works

Printed on film, heat-pressed

DTF Advantage

Newest method, vivid output

Technique 04

Direct to Garment

A printer for t-shirts. Unlimited colors, zero setup.

Best For

Complex art, 1-50 pieces

No Setup Cost

Same quality at any quantity

How It Works

Water-based ink onto fabric

Best Fabric

100% cotton ideal

Technique 05

Pad Printing

The secret behind every branded pen and mug you own.

Best For

Pens, golf balls, bottles

Precision

Sharp on tiny curved surfaces

How It Works

Silicone pad lifts & deposits ink

Colors

1-4 spot, one pass per color

Technique 06

Sublimation

Ink becomes gas, bonds with polyester. Edge-to-edge, zero texture.

Best For

All-over prints, jerseys, mugs

Feel

No hand feel - ink IS the fabric

How It Works

Heat converts ink to gas

Limitation

Polyester only, no dark fabrics

Technique 07

Laser Engraving

Permanent etch into metal, wood, or glass. The premium choice.

Best For

Executive gifts, awards, pens

Permanence

Can't fade or wear away. Ever.

How It Works

Laser at microscopic precision

Premium Feel

Tactile depth conveys quality
